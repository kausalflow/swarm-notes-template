title: Factors Supporting Explanation Learning
---

**Background:** Human understanding of AI decisions, often proxied by forward simulation accuracy in controlled studies, is assumed to be directly proportional to the functional correctness of the provided explanations.

**Question / Future Work:** The study found that correctness alone was not sufficient to guarantee understanding, as many participants failed to learn the decision pattern even with fully correct explanations, necessitating an investigation into what other factors, beyond functional correctness, support learning from explanations.

**Why It Matters:** Identifying these necessary additional factors is crucial for developing XAI evaluation frameworks that move beyond functional metrics to ensure explanations actually foster comprehension.

**Evidence:** Given that correctness alone did not suffice for understanding, identifying what other factors support learning from explanations is equally important.
