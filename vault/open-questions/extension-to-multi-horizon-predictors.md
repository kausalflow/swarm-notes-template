title: Probing multi-horizon predictors
---

**Background:** Temporal models are often trained to predict future states from sequences of past and present observations, making them suitable for analyzing time-lagged causal structures. Extending interpretability frameworks to these models is crucial for domain applications.

**Question / Future Work:** Extend the Causal-INSIGHT framework to analyze multi-horizon predictors, which forecast multiple future time steps simultaneously, rather than just the immediate next step.

**Why It Matters:** Many modern temporal prediction tasks involve generating long sequences or forecasting several steps ahead, requiring the interpretability framework to handle multi-horizon prediction architectures.

**Evidence:** Future work includes extending the probing framework to multi-horizon predictors and developing adaptive clamping schemes to better capture distributed or multi-lag influence patterns.
