title: Advanced modified gravity parameterizations
---

**Background:** The constraint precision on the modified gravity parameter depends on the model used to parameterize deviations from General Relativity (GR).

**Question / Future Work:** Future work should explore and utilize modified gravity parameterizations beyond simple linear rescalings of the gravitational constant, as the current precision achieved (around 3%) may not fully exploit the constraining power of upcoming surveys. This motivation stems from the current constraints being limited by this simple parameterization choice.

**Why It Matters:** Using a more complex or physically motivated parameterization could allow for tighter or more physically meaningful constraints on specific modified gravity theories, which is the ultimate goal of the gravitational redshift probe.

**Evidence:** Finally, our analysis indicates that simple linear rescalings of the gravitational constant are not especially informative as a parameterisation of modified gravity in this context: even under optimistic assumptions, the best constraints we obtain are at the 3% level. This motivates exploring more modified gravity parameterisations (e.g. Narimani and Scott, 2013; Christodoulou and Kazanas, 2019; Jaber et al., 2022), with the aim of making fuller use of the constraining power of upcoming surveys.
