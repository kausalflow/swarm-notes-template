title: Self-consistent modified gravity simulations
---

**Background:** The gravitational redshift signal is derived from the cluster's gravitational potential, which is modeled based on General Relativity (GR) assumptions in this study.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work should involve developing and utilizing large-volume, high-resolution simulations that self-consistently model structure formation and member galaxy populations within viable modified gravity theories that are consistent with gravitational wave constraints (i.e., surviving GW170817). This is necessary because current simulations rely on GR-based halo catalogs, which neglects back-reactions of modified gravity on structure formation.

**Why It Matters:** Current analysis uses GR-based halo catalogues, which means the structural properties of halos themselves (which influence the gravitational redshift signal) are not being tested under the modified gravity scenarios being investigated, limiting the model's predictive power in a full modified gravity universe.

**Evidence:** Our results also underscore the need for high-resolution modified-gravity simulations in large volumes: departures from GR affect not only the gravitational redshift signal but also the halo population itself, which in this work is still drawn from a GR-based catalogue. In this context, it is crucial to develop halo catalogues in modified gravity that are populated self-consistently with member galaxies.
