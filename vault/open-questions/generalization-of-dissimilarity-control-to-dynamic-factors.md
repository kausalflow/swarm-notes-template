title: Generalizing Dissimilarity Control
---

**Background:** Optimal sensor placement strategies are often required to maximize the predictive accuracy of environmental phenomena modeled by spatial statistics like Kriging.

**Question / Future Work:** The paper introduces a persistent coverage control algorithm guided by a dissimilarity map derived from a Kriging model, but a clear path is not established for generalizing this approach to include diverse dynamic environmental factors beyond the modeled solar irradiance fields, such as time-varying sensor noise characteristics or complex, non-stationary spatial dependencies.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding how to incorporate dynamic, real-world sensor characteristics or complex spatial models into the dissimilarity-based control framework is essential for practical deployment.

**Evidence:** The proposed approach was shown to obtain more accurate predictions than the considered baselines under various emulated irradiance fields.
