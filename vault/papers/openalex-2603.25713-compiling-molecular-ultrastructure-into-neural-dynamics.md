# Compiling molecular ultrastructure into neural dynamics

**Authors**: Konrad P. Körding, Anton Arkhipov, Davy Deng, Sean Escola, Seth G. N. Grant, Gal Haspel, Michał Januszewski, Narayanan Kasthuri, Nina Khera, Richie E. Kohman, Grace Lindsay, Jeantine E. **Date**: 2026-03-26
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2603.25713](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25713)

## Summary

This paper proposes the 'ultrastructure-to-dynamics compiler' as a novel framework to translate high-resolution, molecularly annotated ultrastructural brain data into functional physiological parameters needed for biophysical simulations. The compiler is framed as a learned mapping system that requires paired training data consisting of ultrastructure images and corresponding dynamical experimental responses. By successfully implementing this, the authors aim to transform structure-to-function relationships from static descriptions into predictive models of circuit dynamics. This methodology is expected to enhance understanding of neural computation and improve the forecasting of intervention effects within brain circuits.

## Key Contributions

- Introduced the concept of an ultrastructure-to-dynamics compiler, a learned mapping from molecularly annotated ultrastructure to simulator-ready physiological parameters.
- Outlined the requirement for paired training data: jointly acquired ultrastructure from imaging and dynamical responses from physiological experiments.
- Shifting the structure-to-function paradigm from purely descriptive anatomical programs to predictive models of circuit dynamics.
- Opening new avenues for understanding neural computation and forecasting the effects of interventions using structure-derived dynamics.

## Limitations

The approach is contingent on the availability of paired training data, specifically requiring both ultrastructural imaging and corresponding dynamical physiological recordings.

## Key Concepts

- [[ultrastructure-to-dynamics-compiler]]: A learned mapping system that translates high-resolution, molecularly annotated ultrastructural data into parameters suitable for biophysical neural simulators.
- [[molecularly-annotated-ultrastructure]]: High-resolution neuroimaging data that includes the precise location and molecular composition of neural elements, such as synapses.
